Boston, Massachusetts's capital and largest city, is one of the oldest and most historically significant cities in the United States.

Founded in 1630 by Puritan settlers from England, Boston was a central hub during the American Revolution, marked by iconic events such as the Boston Tea Party and the Battle of Bunker Hill.

Located in the northeastern United States, on the coast of the Atlantic Ocean, Boston serves as the cultural and economic hub of the Greater Boston metropolitan area.

As of 2023, the city's population stands at approximately 675,000, while the Greater Boston area boasts a population of around 4.8 million, making it one of the most populous regions in the country.

Boston is renowned worldwide for its higher education, research, and innovation, hosting prestigious institutions like Harvard, MIT, Boston University, and Northeastern University.

The city's economy is diverse, with key sectors including education, healthcare, biotechnology, finance, and information technology. Boston's vibrant start-up culture is a global hub for life sciences and technological innovation.
